Urban areas face significant challenges in managing energy efficiently, exacerbating environmental impacts and straining urban infrastructure. The Sustainable Urban Energy Management system aims to enhance energy efficiency, reduce reliance on non-renewable energy sources, and promote sustainable urban development.

Algorithms Used in Sustainable Energy Management

  • Bellman-Ford Algorithm: Calculates shortest paths for energy flow in a weighted graph with negative weights.
  • Merge Sort: Sorts energy usage or consumption data for analysis and optimization.
  • Bubble Sort: Used to sort simple energy efficiency metrics for demonstration purposes.
  • Selection Sort: Sorts renewable energy data by priority or efficiency.
  • Dynamic Programming (Knapsack): Optimizes energy storage and distribution strategies.
  • Greedy Technique: Allocates energy resources to maximize efficiency or minimize costs.
  • Hashing: Stores and retrieves energy usage data efficiently for real-time monitoring.
