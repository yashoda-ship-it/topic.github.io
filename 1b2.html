The city faces challenges in managing street lighting efficiently, leading to high energy consumption, operational costs, and environmental impacts. The Smart Street Lighting system aims to enhance sustainability and inclusivity by incorporating automated controls and sensors for optimal illumination based on real-time conditions.

Dynamic Scheduling Algorithm

To optimize lighting schedules and minimize energy wastage, a Dynamic Scheduling Algorithm is used. This algorithm takes sensor data as input and adjusts the intensity of streetlights based on factors such as:

  • Traffic density
  • Pedestrian activity
  • Ambient light conditions

The core focus includes:

  • Reducing energy consumption during low-traffic periods
  • Ensuring safety and accessibility in critical zones
  • Integrating renewable energy sources for sustainable operations

Key Components

  • Input: Traffic flow, pedestrian count, ambient light levels, and urban planning data
  • Output: Dynamic lighting schedules and urban infrastructure optimization plans
  • Algorithm: Dynamic Scheduling Algorithm with sensor integration and urban planning considerations
