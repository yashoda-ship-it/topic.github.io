Business Case: Smart Street Lighting

Optimizing Street Lighting for Sustainable Cities

Sub-task: Energy-Efficient Lighting Control

Sustainable Development Goal: SDG Goal 11

Target: 11.6

Indicator: 11.6.1

The city faces challenges in managing street lighting efficiently, leading to high energy consumption and operational costs. The Smart Street Lighting system aims to enhance energy efficiency by incorporating automated controls and sensors for optimal illumination based on real-time conditions. To optimize lighting schedules and minimize energy wastage, a Dynamic Scheduling Algorithm is used. This algorithm takes sensor data as input and adjusts the intensity of streetlights based on factors such as traffic density, pedestrian activity, and ambient light conditions. The core focus includes:

  • Reducing energy consumption during low-traffic periods.
  • Ensuring safety in critical zones.
  • Integrating renewable energy sources for sustainable operations.
Key Components:
  • Input: Traffic flow, pedestrian count, and ambient light levels.
  • Output: Dynamic lighting schedules.
  • Algorithm: Dynamic Scheduling Algorithm with sensor integration.
