Business Case: Smart Street Lighting

Optimizing Urban Lighting Systems

Sub-task: Sustainable and Inclusive Urban Infrastructure

SDG Goal: 11

Target: 11.6

Indicator: 11.6.1

The city faces challenges in managing street lighting efficiently, leading to high energy consumption, operational costs, and environmental impacts. The Smart Street Lighting system aims to enhance sustainability and inclusivity by incorporating automated controls and sensors for optimal illumination based on real-time conditions.

The Dynamic Scheduling Algorithm optimizes lighting schedules to minimize energy wastage based on sensor data, including traffic density, pedestrian activity, and ambient light conditions. Key components include:

  • Input: Traffic flow, pedestrian count, ambient light levels, and urban planning data
  • Output: Dynamic lighting schedules and urban infrastructure optimization plans
  • Algorithm: Dynamic Scheduling Algorithm with sensor integration and urban planning considerations

Reflections: Further improvements could involve predictive analytics, integrating smart poles with multi-purpose features, and collaborating with urban planners for sustainable strategies.

Algorithms used:

  • Prim's Algorithm: Finds the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) of a weighted graph, connecting all vertices with the minimum possible total edge weight.
  • Bellman-Ford Algorithm: Computes the shortest paths from a single source vertex to all other vertices in a graph, even with negative weight edges.
  • Quick Sort: A divide-and-conquer sorting algorithm that sorts an array by partitioning it around a pivot.
  • Selection Sort: A simple sorting algorithm that repeatedly selects the smallest element and places it in its correct position.
  • AVL Tree: A self-balancing binary search tree that ensures logarithmic time complexity for insertions and deletions.
  • Fenwick Tree (Binary Indexed Tree): A data structure for efficiently performing cumulative frequency queries and updates.
  • Trie: A tree-based data structure optimized for fast insertion and search of strings, used here for managing lighting zones.
