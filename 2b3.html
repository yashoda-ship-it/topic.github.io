Self-Driving Cars Solutions

1. Route Optimization with Graph Algorithms

Use Dijkstra’s Algorithm for real-time route calculations. This algorithm ensures safe and efficient navigation by finding the shortest path from the car’s current location to its destination, considering obstacles and road conditions in real-time.

Employ the Floyd-Warshall Algorithm to precompute all-pairs shortest paths for robust navigation maps. This approach ensures that the self-driving car can quickly adapt to changes in the environment, providing a comprehensive navigation map that is updated dynamically based on real-time sensor data.

2. Obstacle Detection with Sorting and Searching

Use Quick Sort to prioritize obstacles by proximity. This sorting algorithm allows the self-driving car to quickly identify and address immediate threats, ensuring safe navigation through urban environments.

Employ Binary Search to quickly check maps for known hazards. This searching technique efficiently locates obstacles within the map, helping the car avoid hazards and navigate safely.

3. Decision Making with Dynamic Programming

Apply the Knapsack Problem to balance energy efficiency, travel time, and passenger preferences. This dynamic programming approach enables the self-driving car to make optimal decisions regarding route selection based on multiple criteria, such as fuel efficiency and passenger comfort.
